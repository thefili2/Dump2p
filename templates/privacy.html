Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2025

Data Controller

This service is operated by an independent developer.

Architecture & Data Processed

Dump p2p utilizes a hybrid architecture. Data handling differs based on the feature used:

1. Main Chat (Server-Backed)

  • Messages: Transmitted through and temporarily stored on our server to provide chat history. Messages are automatically permanently deleted after 24 hours.
  • Nickname & Tag: Stored temporarily in the database to show "Online Users". Deleted when you leave or close the tab.

2. Private Rooms, Voice, & Files (Peer-to-Peer)

  • Messages, Audio, and Files: Transmitted directly between browsers using WebRTC. This data never touches our servers.
  • IP Address: Temporarily transmitted to the PeerJS signaling server (third party) solely to establish the initial P2P connection. Not logged or retained by us.
  • Microphone Access: Requested locally by your browser only when you explicitly click "Join Voice" in a private room. Audio is never recorded or stored.

Purpose of Processing

Data is processed exclusively to enable the chat, voice, and file sharing services to function.

Legal Basis

Processing is based on user consent. By using the service, you accept this privacy policy (Art. 6(1)(a) of the GDPR).

Third Parties

We use the public PeerJS signaling server (peerjs.com) to enable the initial connection between peers for Private Rooms and Voice. This service is operated by a third party and is subject to its own privacy policy.

Data Retention

Main Chat: Messages are automatically purged from the database after 24 hours. Online status is deleted immediately upon leaving.

Private Rooms/Voice: No data is retained on our end. Data exists only in the browsers' temporary memory during an active session and disappears when the page is closed.

Cookies & Local Storage

This service does not use cookies. It uses browser `sessionStorage` to temporarily remember your anonymous tag during a single tab session, which is deleted when the tab is closed.

Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority in your EU member state.
